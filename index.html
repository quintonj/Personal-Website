The Home Depot

Worked as a front-end developer during a summer internship for
The Home Depot.

Over the course of summer 2017, I was a Software Engineering Intern for The Home Depot. Within a group of seven interns, I developed the front-end for a data analytics web application for the Contact Center Experience Team. The application aggregates and displays common categories that customers chat about with Home Depot Support. Users of the application can narrow down their results by applying filters to categories they select and ultimately reach individual chat transcripts to understand customers' problems. To develop the front-end, I utilized HTML, CSS, and JavaScript, as well as React.js as a front-end library. To better understand the needs of the customers and support agents, I observed a Home Depot call center and sat in on user interviews. In addition to this, I participated in several design studios to create lo-fidelity mockups for the application. I also collaborated with fellow interns and mentors to ideate features for the project and to hold a design critique.

  • Date: May 2017 - August 2017
  • Languages Used: HTML, CSS, JavaScript
  • Tools Used: React.js, Visual Studio, Pivotal Cloud Foundry
  • Category: Front-End Development, User Interface Design, User Experience

Atlanta Street
Art Project

An Android application that allows users to explore street art
throughout Atlanta.

This application was designed and developed over the course of one year within a group of five. It began as a capstone project and was continued as a side project so that I could see it through to completion. The project began with user research along with sketching out potential design ideas. After lo-fidelity mockups were created, we conducted usability tests to ensure that our designs were on the right path. After this, I took the critiques from the tests and developed a hi-fidelity prototype with Sketch and InVision. Once we had a full design, we started the implementation process. I have since also designed a logo for the application and have had it published to the Google Play Store. For a more detailed overview of the design process, be sure to read more on Behance.

STAR

An Android application that allows users who are deaf or hard of hearing to communicate with others quickly through the use of speech-to-text.

STAR allows users who are deaf or hard of hearing to utilze speech-to-text and text-to-speech in order to hold conversations with others. It is meant as a bridge for communication between the users and those who do not know American Sign Language. The application acts as a standard messaging app, where users can type what they would like to say into the text field. The person they are talking to can then use the microphone to respond to the user. The application also uses location as a tool for context awareness. The presets at the bottom of the interface are dynamic depending on the user's surrounding area. Finally, users can have multiple conversations available to them and their message history is persistent. Thus, if they are in the same location again, they can pull up their old messages so that they do not have to repeat the process.

  • Date: Spring 2017
  • Source Code: Found on Github
  • Languages Used: Java, XML
  • Tools Used: Android Studio
  • Category: Mobile Development, User Interface Design

Guide to Brewing Coffee

A poster design based off of user research that describes how to
brew a cup of coffee.

This poster design was based off of several ethnographic observation sessions with a coffee enthusiast. During these sessions, I wrote notes, drew sketches, and took pictures to understand the process.
